Masque of the Red Death

by Sara Deck for Fright Rags

I had to have this pin when I spied on the Fright Rags site a few months ago. Granted, it took me about a second to realize that it was based on Lon Chaney's performance, rather than Roger Corman's film, which starred Vincent Price, Hazel Court (who I always confuse for Carol Ohmart), and it girl Jane Asher. Even though, I have seen the latter multiple times and not once does Vincent Price look like this, but still..

I quickly rectified my ignorance by checking out the Youtube clip of Lon is all his opulence.

The pin is really lovely. It could only be more grand - and terrifingly tacky - if it were bejeweled in red gemstones.

I find that with pins I am not at all a fan of high delineation, particularly of faces. Apart from the feathers, which get a little lost due to the size, the lines are effective in the skull - where, in combination with the lighter hue, they make the skull pop. I only wish that the face, hands, and staff head glowed in the dark. But, this is pretty close to perfection.

It also only has one pin in the back that is not too big, but I wouldn't wear it with a tight knit.

It is a first pressing, though I saw nothing to indicate it on the card or pin itself. It measures about two inches high and one and a half inches at its widest. The back has an elevated Fright Rags logo.

Vincent Price

by Mitch O'Connell for Creepy Co.

I gushed when I opened the package and glanced at that beautiful, blue visage. Vincent Price "Phosphorescent Pallor" is even handsomer in person than the site photos had me believe. I thought of Shelby Carpenter, that fragile, selfish, pretty boy that he played in Laura. That furrowed brow is not scaring me.

The hair and edges have the most attention to line but in a way that is not overwhelming - thanks to the flatness of the pin. Parts of him also glow in the dark in a soft, haunting hue - the face and outer edges of his hair, i.e. the white parts.

He is only slightly bigger than the Lon Chaney pin but feels a lot heavier in hand. A quick turn on its side, and it seems to be at least a third thicker than the latter. The back is stamped with Creepy Co. and features two diagonal pins and rubber stoppers.

I bought two additional enamel pins from the company, and am super-impressed by the quality and aesthetic of all, even this simple cutie.

This measures about two inches by one and half inches. It is a limited edition pin. There is a human-colored version. And, there had been a Dr. Phibes-inspired pink one as well as (this hurts to even type) a Masque of the Red Death (badly sunburnt) version.

Hello

by Haasbroek for Tee Fury

Proof that my pop culture dalliances are not just with the horrific and dead. I bought this, along with another pin, last winter. I had seen the shirt version, which has the added "Is it me you're looking for" tagline, on a few sites ages before and felt a weak longing for it. (But, I seriously dislike wearing white shirts.)

It's small - a mere 1.25 by 1 5/16 inches (at its widest). Light, and only has one centrally located pin on the back with a rubber stopper. The back also has the series number.

TeeFury had a small batch of pins, and seemed to be replenishing those that were sold out with new designs. While they did appear on a limited edition part of their site, I didn't think that they were going to do away with all of them. Hopefully, it's temporary; and, we'll eventually see an "I Snake NY" pin.

Phantom of the Paradise

by Pop-ocalypse from Zazzle

I buy a lot of stationary and office-type goods on Zazzle. I will randomly search for a favorite film and come up pretty empty - until this pretty little pin-back button.

As always, I underestimated the size. I should have gone with the 2 1/4", instead of the 1 1/4", to get the full effect of the still. The black and purple background get lost - especially under my crappy indoor lighting.

What's there to say? It's unassumingly attractive, lightweight, and properly manufactured. Plus, the thinness of the pin enables someone, who cares about their clothes, to wear them with tighter knits. I wish it would have been offered as other products on the site, though.

I should add that apart from size options, you can choose between a round or square button.

The Morning After

by Paul Southworth for Threadless

I bought the Morning After pin for a young family relative. Although not quite Emo or Goth, a vomiting, Bozo-like clown is amusing enough without the usual, tasteless JWG reference.

The pin seems a little small. It measures about an inch high and 1 1/8" at its widest. Granted, I knew the measurements before buying it, but, in hand, it seemed like one of the many old school pins that you'd find on a musty, tobacco-laden beret. For all obnoxious purposes, I think it should have been about 2" high.

Apart from that, I have no complaints. It looks exactly like the illustration. defect-free, and has one small pin in the back - that I still wouldn't wear with a nice sweater.

It is limited to 100. The enamel is shallow while thick, dark lines are raised around it.
